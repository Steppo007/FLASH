## Tile

### Overview

**Tiles** are used to display information or represent content in a tile or list format. They can contain various types of content, including text, images, or icons. Tiles serve as an organized and visually appealing way to present data, links, or actions to users.

* * * * *

### When to Use It

-   To display content in a grid or list format.

-   To present information, links, or actions in an organized and visually appealing manner.

-   To create a dashboard or overview page with multiple pieces of content.

#### **Avoid using tiles when:**

-   The content is better suited for a different layout, such as a table or form.

-   The information does not require a visual representation.

-   The tile format does not enhance the user experience or clarity of the content.

### Variants

-   **List**: Tiles arranged in a list format, often used for displaying items in a vertical layout.

-   **Grid**: Tiles arranged in a grid format, often used for displaying items in a more compact and organized layout.

* * * * *

### Behavior

-   **Default**: The tile displays the content with appropriate styling and layout.

-   **Hover**: The tile icon changes color to indicate it is interactive and can be clicked.

-   **Greyed Out**: The tile appears grayed out and is non-interactive, indicating it is not currently relevant or available.

### Dos and Don'ts

#### **Dos:**

✔ Use tiles to organize and present content in a visually appealing way.\
✔ Ensure tiles are easily distinguishable by content type and purpose.\
✔ Provide clear and concise labels or descriptions for the content within each tile.

#### **Don'ts:**

✘ Don't use tiles for content that is better suited for a different layout.\
✘ Don't overload tiles with too much information or content.\
✘ Don't rely solely on color to indicate interactivity---use additional visual cues like hover effects.
